Quest information[edit]

Objectives[edit]

  • Speak with Ventari
  • Defeat the forces surrounding Ventari's Refuge.
  • Return to Councilor Vaylor for your reward.

Reward[edit]

  • 500 Experience
  • Inscribed Chakram
Energy +10 (Requires 8 Domination Magic)
Halves casting time of spells (Chance: 5%)
Armor +5 (vs. elemental damage)

Walkthrough[edit]

Go into Ventari's Refuge and speak with Ventari. He is an old centaur who has become a pacifist, and will offer no help in fighting the White Mantle or anything else. Return outside and fight off the three groups of Mantle that have caught up with you.

All three groups consist of 2 White Mantle Knights and 1 White Mantle Sycophant. You will also get help from 3 groups of Shining Blade, consisting of a Shining Blade Elementalist, Shining Blade Mesmer and a Shining Blade Necromancer. Once the White Mantle are defeated, return to Councilor Vaylor and claim the reward.

Dialogue[edit]

Initial dialogue[edit]

Councilor Vaylor

"I need you to speak with Ventari. He will not allow me into his refuge. It is a long story and I do not wish to discuss it now. I know that he prefers to remain neutral, but the White Mantle and their masters are a threat to all Tyrians, not just Krytans, and not just humans. This is no mere civil war. There is much more at stake here than most of us know. More than we'd believe, I imagine.
Ventari and his jungle-dwelling friends can help us, if they choose to do so. Speak with him, I pray you, and return to me."
 Accept: "I'll talk to him."
 Decline: "I think you should send someone else."
 Ask: "I fear we will need help to overcome these foes."

Reward dialogue[edit]

Councilor Vaylor

"Valiantly fought! The Shining Blade are lucky to have you on our side. There is more at stake than just these camps of ours. A short time ago we have discovered an ancient druid ritual to unlock the gates of Denravi. If we have been betrayed they know of our plans for certain. You must warn the people of Aurora Glade about the coming White Mantle offensive. I pray we're not too late."
