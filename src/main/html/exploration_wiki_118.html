Sunqua Vale is an idyllic location nestled between the mountains and the southwestern cliffs of Shing Jea Island. It includes portions of the short east-west mountain range of which Tomaat Pass leads from the Shrine of Maat towards the western coast of the island. The lowland areas of this area are sparsely populated by simple Mantids and wandering Kappa. Towards the western end, near Lake Puang, some Naga can be encountered. The snowy mountain range is slightly more dangerous, with bands of Sensali Tengu and the Crimson Skull patrolling about.

This is the first explorable area where new players will encounter hostile enemies. The Mantids (excluding a few in Zhengjo Temple), Kappa, and Naga in the area are passive until attacked first, but the Sensali, the Yetis, and the Crimson skull band encountered in the northern mountainous paths do attack on aggro.

Getting there[edit]

Exit from either Shing Jea Monastery, Minister Cho's Estate, or Tsumei Village. Quite a few quests from the monastery will lead you out here.

Shrines[edit]

Resurrection shrines with a statue
  • Avatar of Dwayna
    • East, at the exit into Shing Jea Monastery.
  • Champion of Balthazar
    • North, at the exit into Minister Cho's Estate.
  • Lyssa's Muse
    • In the snowy mountainous area, east of the Shrine of Maat.
  • Melandru's Watcher
    • Southwest, at the exit into Tsumei Village.
  • Voice of Grenth
    • West, at the exit into Kinya Province.

Notes[edit]

  • These quests will affect the spawns in this area:
    • A Formal Introduction
    • Appearance of the Naga
    • Cleansing the Shrine
    • Forming a Party
    • Free the Fur
    • Little Creatures
    • Minions Gone Wild
    • Opening Strike
    • Spark of Interest
    • Stale Mate
    • Study Buddy
    • The Siege at Tsumei Village
  • These festival quests will also affect the spawns in this area:
    • A Strange Request
    • Fire in the Sky
    • Just My Luck
    • The Shrine of Maat
  • Complete exploration of this area adds approximately 3.3% to the Canthan Cartographer title track.
  • Vanquisher runs of this area without active quests require defeating around 135 foes; the total can range from 105 to 152, including quest influences.
    • The fewest number of foes spawn during A Strange Request, which is part of the Dragon Festival.
    • Several Kappa will jump out of the waters, if you approach too close the southeastern waterfront.
    • Similarly, a few Naga will show up as you cross the bridge over Lake Puang.
    • A single, hidden Lesser Oni can be found a short distance northwest from the Tsumei Village portal.
    • The Kappa and Mantids are hostile to each other, though they will only fight in Hard mode.
